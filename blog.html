A Week of German Lunches

Every day last week I had lunch with a different German speaker at Babbel. Mega thanks to Nikki, David, Nicole, Carla, and Catha!

Did I have fun? Yes, the kind of fun you’re having the first time you drive a car like “What if I crash but OH the thrill of operating a vehicle!”

The best thing I heard myself say during the last lunch:

Ich habe neunundneunzig Probleme, und ein Mann ist nicht Eins.

Though apparently there are a few ways to say this.

Thank you again, German speakers!

Going For a Walk?

I went to Norway last week and stayed in a small house in the middle of Skulsfjord, which is Almost Nowhere.

The other guest in this house was a French gal. She could speak Spanish, Italian, French of course, and a little English, but only a little.

A natural extrovert, I want to continue to chat about our plans for the day. On our second morning, I ask her "Sprechen Sie Deutsch?" She smiled (a little nervously): "Ja, ein bisschen."

Thrilled, I decided we would continue our conversation in German. I lauched into:

"Gehen Sie heute für ein Laufen?"

Her smile faded (nooooooo!) and she tilted her head. "Perhaps, onward with English still?"

It was later that I learned the correct way to phrase my question.

"Gehst du heute spazieren?"

And now I know. Though perhaps next time I'll clarify first that "wir Düzen uns."

Valentines

As part of my German learning, I sent Valentines.

Babbel allows you to send anonymous (or not) cards to people on the (two) days leading up to Valentines Day.

Do I participate? Yes I do, I love this kitschy stupid holiday, pipe down V-day haters.

WRITE ALL THE CARDS FOR EVERYONE

But really, that was part of our assignment - send someone else in the group a Valentine “auf Deutsch.”

I perused our chatroom for members that seemed to have a nugget of enthusiasm about this thing and selected three people to send a fully (broken) German poem to.

Small problem, I forgot what I wrote. So if you're reading this, and you got a card from me, and you wouldn't mind my sharing it as my NEXT post (it belongs to you after all), let me know?
